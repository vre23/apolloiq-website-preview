Quick Start Guide

Follow these simple steps to start using ScribeCraft in your consultations.

1. Log into Merlin

Access ScribeCraft through your dedicated account on the Merlin platform. Navigate to merlin.apolloiq.co.uk and sign in with your credentials. No software to install — ScribeCraft runs entirely in your browser.

TipScribeCraft works on Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari — on any device with a microphone and internet connection.

2. Set Up Your Microphone

Before your first session, ensure your microphone is configured correctly:

  1. Select your microphone — Click the microphone icon next to the Start button and choose your preferred device from the list.
  2. Test audio capture — Verify the microphone picks up your voice clearly at a comfortable distance.
  3. Grant browser permissions — When prompted, click Allow to let ScribeCraft access your microphone. If you accidentally blocked it, click the lock icon in the address bar and change the Microphone setting to Allow.
TipA USB desk microphone gives the best audio quality. Your laptop's built-in microphone works well for short distances. Position the microphone 30-50 cm from the speaker for optimal results.

3. Choose a Note Format

Select from the available clinical templates — SOAP notes, clinical letters, referral letters, or specialty-specific formats. Choosing the right template before recording helps ScribeCraft structure information exactly the way you need it.

4. Obtain Patient Consent

Before starting the recording, inform the patient and obtain their consent. This is a legal requirement under UK data protection law and good clinical practice.

Suggested phrasing: "I'll be using a digital assistant that transcribes our conversation to generate clinical notes. Are you happy with that?"

ImportantPatient consent is mandatory under UK GDPR. Ensure the patient understands and agrees before recording the consultation.

5. Start Recording

Press the Start button to begin recording. Conduct your consultation naturally — ScribeCraft works in the background without interrupting your clinical workflow. It adapts to your natural conversation flow.

6. Stop and Process

When the consultation ends, click Stop. ScribeCraft will automatically process the conversation and generate a structured clinical note according to your chosen template. Processing usually takes just a few seconds.

7. Review and Edit

Read the generated draft carefully. Check that all clinical details are accurate — especially medication doses, numerical values, and diagnoses. Edit directly in the editor if any corrections are needed.

ImportantAlways review the generated note before using it officially. ScribeCraft produces draft documents — final clinical responsibility remains with you.

8. Copy and File

Once you're satisfied with the note:

  • Copy to your EPR — Paste the note directly into EMIS Web, SystmOne, Vision, or any EPR system.
  • Export as PDF — Generate a professional document for printing or patient records.
TipNotes are saved automatically in your Merlin account. You can return to any previous note from the History section at any time.

Accuracy Optimisation

Tips for achieving the most precise transcriptions and clinical notes.

Understanding Accuracy

Transcription accuracy is the foundation of every clinical note generated by ScribeCraft. Accurate transcription ensures the final note faithfully reflects the clinician-patient dialogue. ScribeCraft uses AI models trained on medical terminology, capable of recognising thousands of clinical terms, drug names from the BNF, and specialty-specific vocabulary.

Average accuracy exceeds 96% in optimal conditions, but can be significantly influenced by factors you can control. The difference between 94% and 98% accuracy in a typical 15-minute consultation generating approximately 2,000 words means the difference between 120 potentially incorrect words and only 40.

Microphone Positioning

Audio quality is the most important technical factor affecting transcription accuracy.

Face-to-face consultations

  • USB desk microphone — Position 30-50 cm away, oriented towards the centre between clinician and patient. Place on a stable surface to reduce vibrations.
  • Lapel microphone — Clip to your lapel, approximately 15-20 cm from your mouth. Ideal for noisy consulting rooms.
  • Laptop microphone — Keep the laptop open at 90-120 degrees with no obstructions between the microphone and speaker. Ideal distance is 40-60 cm.

Home visits

Use a lapel microphone connected to your phone or tablet. Place the recording device on a stable surface — not on your lap or on the patient's bed.

TipDo a 30-second test before each clinic session. Record a short phrase and check the preview to verify the transcription is accurate.

Optimising Your Recording Environment

The acoustic environment significantly impacts transcription quality. Background noise, reverberation, and interference are the main enemies of accurate transcription.

Reducing background noise

  1. Air conditioning — Turn off or reduce to minimum during consultations, or position the microphone as far as possible from the unit.
  2. Waiting room noise — Ensure the consulting room door is fully closed. A door seal significantly reduces transmitted noise.
  3. Medical equipment — Schedule noisy equipment use outside recording periods where possible.
  4. Phone notifications — Enable silent mode on your phone during consultations.
ImportantDo not try to compensate for ambient noise by speaking louder. Raised voice distorts the sound and can reduce accuracy. It is always more effective to reduce noise at the source.

Speaking Clearly

You don't need to change your natural communication style — just avoid a few common pitfalls:

  • Normal conversational pace — Speak as you would on the phone with a colleague.
  • Brief pauses at transitions — When moving between sections (e.g., history to examination), pause for 1-2 seconds to help the algorithm identify the transition.
  • Slow down for lists — When listing medications or results, slightly reduce your pace.
  • Dictate numbers clearly — For blood pressure, say each digit: "one three zero over eight five". Specify units immediately: "Metformin, eight hundred and fifty milligrams".

Choosing the Right Template

Templates actively guide the AI algorithm. When you select a template matching your consultation type, ScribeCraft knows what information to expect. A cardiology template prioritises cardiovascular terms; a dermatology template is optimised for lesion descriptions.

ImportantUsing an inappropriate template can reduce accuracy. An ophthalmology template used for a general medicine consultation will prioritise the wrong vocabulary.

Review and Feedback

No automated transcription is 100% accurate. Always review the generated note:

  • Correct words or phrases — Click on text to modify it directly.
  • Add missing details — If information was not captured (due to low volume or overlapping speech), add it manually.
  • Verify critical values — Pay special attention to medication doses, numerical values, and diagnoses.
TipReview clinical notes immediately after each consultation while the information is fresh. Detailed feedback helps us continuously improve ScribeCraft for all users.

Patient Consent

Yes. UK data protection law requires that patients are informed and consent is obtained before recording a consultation. The patient must know:

  • That the consultation will be recorded by an AI tool
  • Why the recording is being made (to generate clinical documentation)
  • Who processes the data and how it is stored
  • That they can decline without any impact on their care
  • Written consent: The patient signs a dedicated form. ApolloIQ provides a customisable consent form template compliant with UK GDPR.
  • Verbal consent: Acceptable if documented in the patient record. Suggested phrasing: "I'll be using a digital assistant that transcribes our conversation to generate clinical notes. Are you happy with that?"
Good practice: Display an informational poster in your waiting room. ApolloIQ provides printable patient-facing materials free of charge.

Accuracy & Limits

ScribeCraft achieves high accuracy rates for medical terminology in optimal recording conditions. Performance varies based on microphone quality, ambient noise, speech clarity, and terminology complexity.

Regardless of accuracy rate, every generated document must be reviewed and validated by you before use.

Yes. As with any NLP technology, potential issues include:

  • Transcription errors: phonetically similar words may be confused
  • Contextual misinterpretation: information may be placed in the wrong template section
  • Omissions: some details mentioned in conversation may be absent from the draft
  • AI hallucinations: in rare cases, information not explicitly mentioned may appear

ScribeCraft operates on the "Human-in-the-Loop" principle: AI is a support tool, not an author. Every generated document is a draft. As a clinician, you have the legal and professional obligation to:

  • Read the entire generated draft
  • Correct any errors identified
  • Validate the document before printing, exporting, or filing
Never print or officially use any ScribeCraft-generated document without personally reading and validating it. Responsibility for the final content is yours.

For Patients

This information can be shared with patients or displayed in your waiting room.

What is ScribeCraft?

ScribeCraft is a digital assistant used by your clinician to help with note-taking during your consultation. It listens to the conversation and creates a written summary for your medical records.

What happens during the consultation?

  • Your clinician will ask for your permission before using ScribeCraft
  • The conversation is recorded by the device's microphone
  • ScribeCraft creates a draft of clinical notes from the conversation
  • Your clinician reviews and checks the notes before they are saved
  • The audio recording is automatically deleted — it is never stored

Is my data safe?

  • Audio recordings are deleted immediately after processing
  • All data is encrypted and securely stored
  • Only your clinician can access the generated notes
  • Your data is never used to train AI systems
  • You can request deletion of your data at any time

Can I refuse?

Absolutely. If you prefer not to have ScribeCraft used during your consultation, simply tell your clinician. Your care will continue exactly as normal. There is no penalty or change in the quality of service.
